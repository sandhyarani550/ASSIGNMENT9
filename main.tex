\section{Question No 2.43}
Solve -3x+2y $\geq$ -6 
%
\section{SOLUTION} 
Let $\myvec{-3 & 2}\vec{x}$ = -6 intersects the x-axis and y-axis at $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$ respectively.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Let $\vec{A}$ = $\myvec{x\\0}$ 
\\
Put $\vec{A}$ in equation 
\begin{align}
\myvec{-3 & 2}\myvec{x \\ 0}=-6
\\
\implies x = 2
\\
\therefore \vec{A}=\myvec{2\\0}
\end{align}
\item Let $\vec{B}$ = $\myvec{0\\y}$
\\
Put $\vec{B}$ in equation 
\begin{align}
\myvec{-3 & 2}\myvec{0 \\ y}=-6
\\
\implies y = -3
\\
\therefore \vec{B}=\myvec{0\\-3}
\end{align}
\item Origin = \myvec{0\\0} satisfy the equation $\myvec{-3 & 2}\vec{x}\geq$-6
\\
$\implies$ The solution is the right side of the line $\myvec{-3 & 2}\vec{x}$ = -6 
\\
\item The following python code is the diagrammatic representation of the solution in Fig. 2.1
\end{enumerate}
Solution of -3x+2y $\geq$ -6
